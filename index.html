Why It's Important

Georgia Tech notes physical wellness as one of its 8 Dimensions of Wellness. This includes replenishing the body through physical activity, sleep, and nutrition.

"Students involved in outdoor recreation have more friends, less social anxiety, and increased social support... all of which can help make their transition to university life easier."
- Andre et al., 2017

A 2008 study of collegiate intramural sports participants showed an increase in 41 out of 41 measured outcome items, noting the greatest gains in sense of belonging, time management, school pride, and leadership development (Haines and Fortman, 2008).

Impact on Retention (Miller and Croft, 2022)

Participants responded on a scale from 1-5 (1 = strongly agree):

  • Return due to sense of belonging: Mean = 1.96
  • Satisfaction with activities offered: Mean = 2.21
  • Creating a network of friends: Mean = 2.46
  • Creating a network of friends: Mean = 2.46
  • Trust in peers: Mean = 2.67
  • Lack of perceived responsibility to the university: Mean = 4.34

1. Couch Field Drainage

Burger and Couch Fields host soccer, football, ultimate frisbee, volleyball, archery club, and various other student organization activities. They are heavily trafficked but located on low-lying ground, causing water accumulation and muddy surfaces, shown in the images to the right.

Permanent Solution: Install subsurface drainage with perforated pipes, gravel layers, and channels to divert water into storm systems. Timeline: 4–6 months.

Temporary Solution: Reduce field usage after heavy rain or events to allow soil recovery. Maintenance crews can reseed, aerate, and perform minor repairs. Timeline: periodic.

Est. Budget $100,000
Timeline 6 Months

2. Peters Deck Lights & Benches

Peters Deck basketball courts are popular but lack lighting and benches. We propose installing overhead LED lights for safe nighttime use and adding benches for resting and socialization. This will improve accessibility, safety, and overall usability. Additionally, we propose repainting the lines as they are another current issue with the courts, as seen in the image to the left.

Est. Budget $37,000
Timeline 3 Months

3. Re-turf Stamps Field

Stamps Field is central to intramural and club sports but its turf is deteriorated, increasing injury risks. We propose re-turfing with high-quality artificial turf for better shock absorption, durability, and safety.

The turf images shown to the right were taken by us. The Stamps Turf shows old, worn turf with faded lines. The newer turf is from a new field in Georgia, and from experience it was bouncy (for shock absorbtion), softer, and had better drawn lines. From experience playing on both fields, the newer turf was like night and day.

Est. Budget $1,980,000
Timeline Summer '24

4. CRC Equipment Update

CRC renovations include updating climbing wall holds, improving safety padding, re-stocking badminton facilities, replacing worn basketballs, and upgrading rowing and cardio machines. These updates modernize facilities, improve safety, and enhance overall student wellness.

Est. Budget $11,000
Timeline Ongoing
